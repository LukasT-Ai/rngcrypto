## The Degen Phase

Everyone starts the same way. You hear about crypto from a friend, a tweet, or a YouTube video. You buy something. It goes up. You think you are a genius. Then it goes down and you realize you have no idea what you are doing.

I went through that cycle multiple times. Buying tokens because the chart looked good. Aping into NFT mints because the Discord was hyped. Watching my portfolio swing 50% in a day and convincing myself that was normal.

The degen phase is not wasted time though. It teaches you what the market actually is: a machine for transferring money from the impatient to the patient, and from the uninformed to the informed.

## The Shift

The turning point was when I started asking "how" instead of "what." Not "what should I buy" but "how does this protocol actually work?" Not "what is pumping" but "how does the market microstructure create these price movements?"

Once you start pulling apart how things work, you cannot go back to blind speculation. You see that every DeFi yield has a source. You understand that NFT floors are a function of holder distribution and listing pressure. You realize that trading is not about being right, it is about having an edge and managing risk.

## Building Instead of Betting

The natural progression from understanding how things work is building things yourself. If you can see that a market is mispriced, why not write a bot to trade it? If you think a protocol is undervalued, why not build tools around it?

I started with simple scripts. Monitoring prices. Tracking whale wallets. Alerting on large transactions. Each script taught me something about the infrastructure: APIs, RPC nodes, websockets, databases.

Then the scripts became systems. A trading bot that scans markets, calculates fair values, manages positions, and reports via Telegram. A website that displays live trading stats. A dashboard that tracks PnL across strategies.

The transition from consumer to builder changed everything. Instead of refreshing CoinGecko hoping for green candles, I was debugging code at 2am because my bot found an edge in a new market. Different kind of obsession, but a productive one.

## The Cardano Ecosystem

I ended up deep in the Cardano ecosystem, not because of ideology but because of opportunity. Cardano DeFi is early enough that a single builder can make an impact. On Ethereum, you are competing with hundreds of well funded teams and thousands of bots. On Cardano, the competition is thinner and the community actually pays attention to what you build.

Liqwid, Minswap, Ascend Market, Midnight. These are the protocols I spend most of my time with. Not as a passive holder, but as someone building tools, testing strategies, and contributing to the ecosystem.

The Cardano community gets a lot of criticism for being too focused on technology and not enough on market dynamics. There is some truth to that. But from a builder's perspective, the technology focus means the infrastructure is solid. When you build on Cardano, things work deterministically. Transactions do not fail because someone front ran you. State does not change between when you read it and when you write to it.

## What I Learned

**Trade what you understand.** If you cannot explain why a position should be profitable, you are gambling. Every trade my bot takes has a specific thesis: the crowd is mispriced relative to the reference, or momentum supports the direction, or cross platform data confirms the edge. No thesis, no trade.

**Automate everything you can.** Humans are bad at executing trading rules consistently. We get emotional. We hold losers too long and cut winners too short. Bots do not have feelings. They execute the rules as written, every time.

**Transparency builds trust.** My trading stats are public at rngcrypto.com. Every trade, every win, every loss. Publishing your results honestly, including the bad trades, earns more credibility than any amount of "trust me bro" alpha calls.

**The community matters more than the tech.** I have used protocols with amazing technology and dead communities. They go nowhere. And I have seen mid tech protocols with passionate communities thrive. Cardano's community, for all its drama, is one of the most engaged in crypto.

## What Is Next

I am heads down building. The trading bot is live and grinding on Ascend testnet. The website tracks everything in real time. YouTube videos document the journey.

The goal is not to become a crypto influencer or sell courses. The goal is to build systems that generate value, share the process openly, and contribute to an ecosystem I believe in.

From degen to builder. The journey continues.
